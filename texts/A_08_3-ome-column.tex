    \ParallelLText{\textit{Ome duyo lo. Duyo klapno bike lu. Bike wepulo yipyo, epe kep sano, ``Ako kopi ari?'' ``Ako depi kero bûri depi woko.'' Yena sûkui pi. Yena pupno woni, yena garamut rino kowe. Yelo niyerip. Yuku yakono sûkui roki pi, Yiyo; sûkui kipiwe. Ki pako kipiyapno pe roise wel. Yena sûkui popno ako kep reyap. Ome ki dob sana, ``Eh ako kopi ke roki ono baya.'' Sûkui roki ipoi. ``Ko pe pakoyo ulei, ko pulapi.'' Biliyo nekipno ki pulapiro.}}
    \ParallelRText{Ome went to the bush and shot a cassowary. He brought the cassowary home and asked his mother: ``Isn't my wife here?'' She replied: ``Your wife, she accompanied your sister.'' The people danced. They blew the conches and they beat the garamut. The ground was shaking. Men and women swayed, at the place Yi; they danced close together. He took a bow and arrows. While the people danced, he looked at his wife. Ome looked there, ``Eh, my wife is dancing with another man.'' They swayed in the dance. ``I will put an arrow on the bow and draw the bow,'' says Ome to himself. As he stands at the entrance, he draws the bow.}
